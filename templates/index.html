Modeling the Outcome of a Pitch With Machine Learning


    Welcome to the Pitch-O-Rama

  • The Wind Up
    This is a predictive module we have put together using Html, Python, Javascript, SQL, Jupyter Notebooks, Flask, AWS, and CSS. We took data from the MLB statcast database for 2020 and 2019, cleaned and sorted the data, then parsed it down to a workable data set used to explore the questions we had about the MLB. We plugged our dataset into a variety of machine learning models to see what is was we could accurately predict, and what sort of module predicted it the best. In the end, and after 93 columns data, our Gradient Boosted Classifier model was able to predict pitch outcomes (Strike, Ball/Hit) with 73% accuracy. Our model and dataset decided, we converted our dataframe into SQL tables and loaded our servers into AWS. Now that our model was complete, we moved on to creating the Website housing that would hold our predictive models and allow users to interact with out dataset. We created a pair of HTML templates, one to house this introduction, and a second with a series of drop-down menus that allow you to choose from any of our dataset features, click a predict button, and our model will let you know the outcome of your hypothetical pitch. The website then calls from an app.py file that references our stored machine learning model, finds the predictive value for the combined inputs, and returns the outcome of your pitch. We hope you enjoy!
